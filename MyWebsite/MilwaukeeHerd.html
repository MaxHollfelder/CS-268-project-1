About the G league

The G league is the NBA's official minor league basketball organization. The league used to be called the National Basketball Development League (NBDL) from 2001-2005 then it was changed to NBA Development League (NBA D-league) from 2005 until 2017 at which time it turned into the NBA G League. Right now the G league had 28 teams of which are single-affiliated or are owned by an NBA team.

Currently the NBA G league had been making a lot of moves to gain more attraction and help players transition from the G league into the NBA. One way that they are helping the G league players transition into the NBA is that they are offering NBA teams to have two two way contracts where they can sign two players that can play on both a G league roster and also on an NBA roster. In recent news the NBA G league has made a huge splash to increase popularity while also helping players transition from minor league basketball into professional. For the 2019-2020 season the G league will be offering select contracts to players who are not yet eligible to enter the NBA draft. since 2006 the NBA made a role that players most at least be 19 years old in order to enter into the NBA draft, what this meant it that players coming out of high school would have to go to college for at least a year before they entered the draft. The new G league contracts offer an alternative route for players who do not want to attend college. The players will be able to participate in a G league season for a contract up to $125,000 then after that season they will be at least 19 years or younger and will be able to enter into the NBA draft.

The Millwaukee Heard

The Heard is the NBA G league affiliate of the Milwaukee Bucks and was established in 2016. The teams location is in Oshkosh Wisconsin where they play their home games at the Menominee Nation Arena.
