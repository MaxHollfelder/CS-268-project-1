Owners

There are a lot of people who are part of the Bucks ownership group However there are four individuals who make up the largest stakes in the team. Individuals are Wes Edens, Marc Lasry, Jamies Dinan, and Mike Fascitelli. In 2014 The Milwaukee Bucks were looking for new owners who would be willing to spend money and create a team the the City would be proud of and rally around. Because of this they we pleased to welcome in two new big inverster Wed Edens and Marc Lasry. Edens and Lasry are hedge fund billionaires and ended up buying the Milwaukee Bucks from $550 Millon and also gained the support of Dinan and Fascitelli. Another owner that I thought was worth noteing is Aaron Rodgers, Rodgers is a Quarterback for the Green Bay Packer a National Football League player Located in Wisconsin and is a very popular figure in Wisconsin. Although Aaron Rodgers does not own a huge part of the Milwaukee Bucks at only 1% fans of sports in the state still liked seeing him becomeing an owner.

General Manager

The current general manager of the Milwaukee Bucks is John Horst. Jon Horst career of as a franchise front office member started in 2007 when he was appointed the Manager of Basketball Operations for the Detroit Pistons. Then in 2008 he was appointed the Director of Basketball Opersstions for the Milwaukee Bucks, which He held untill 2017. After the 2017 season Horst was names the general manager of the Milwaukee Bucks. It did not take Horst long before he collected some hardware as the general manager of the Bucks, being named NBA Executive of the Year in 2019.

President

The current President of the Milwaukee Bucks is Peter Feigin. Feigin has previously help positions at Madison Square Garden, Marquie Jet Partners, and NetJets but when he was offered a position as the President of the Milwaukee basketball team he packed up his bags and went to Milwaukee. As president Feigin has been been instramental in making the team move towards that future. One of the things that he has been instramental in doing it helping find the funding for the Fiserv Forum arena. Feigin was the on who proposed to the Wisconsin Lawmakes for $250 million in public funding so that the Bucks could could remain in Milwaukee and not be relocated to another city.
